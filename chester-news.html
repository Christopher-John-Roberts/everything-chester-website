The Chester Chronicle


The Chester Chronicle is a weekly newspaper covering Chester and the surrounding area. The Chester Chronicle produces three editions of the paper City, Country and Frodsham and Helsby. The Chester Chronicle delivers all the latest local news, sport & entertainment from Chester and Cheshire. The Chester Chronicle newspaper was first established in the 18th century and currently published every Thursday and has a circulation of around 15,800 copies per week in Chester and the surrounding area.


The Chester Standard


The Chester Standard delivers the latest Chester and West Cheshire news, sport and events from Chester in the United Kingdom. The Chester Standard is a weekly free newspaper that is distributed within the Chester area. The Chester Standard is a sister publication to the Chester Evening Leader, and is produced by NWN Media. The UK's Newspaper Society voted the Chester Standard Free Weekly Newspaper of the Year in the North West, North East and Yorkshire 2008. NWN Media’s headquarters are in Mold and the company now employs 250 people in 12 offices spanning the full geography of the group's coverage in Mid and North Wales, Cheshire, Wirral, Shropshire and Herefordshire.

The Chester Talking Newspaper


The Chester Talking Newspaper is a registered charity and has its own recording studio located in Chester city centre. Since 1977 The Chester Talking Newspaper have been recording and distributing local news to hundreds of visually impaired listeners in the Chester and Flintshire area every week. Their newspapers and magazine are digitally recorded each week at the studios in Chester. These recordings are then copied to USB memory sticks, and posted out to their listeners.
